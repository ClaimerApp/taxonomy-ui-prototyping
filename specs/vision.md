# Taxonomy Vision

## The Thesis

Taxonomy is building the infrastructure for **proactive tax advisory**.

Rather than helping advisors work faster on tasks they already know about, Taxonomy uncovers **important signals hidden within the vast amounts of data that firms already hold and interact with**.

In simple terms:

> Taxonomy continuously analyses a firm’s data and alerts advisors when something important has happened, is about to happen, or is happening right now that they should act on.

These alerts are produced by **Sensors and Interceptors** within the platform, which generate **Signals** when meaningful changes, risks, or opportunities are detected.

- **Sensors** continuously monitor data sources and detect changes over time.
- **Interceptors** operate within workflows and perform last-mile checks before actions are completed.

Signals may represent:

- A **revenue-generating advisory opportunity**
- A **regulatory or legislative change affecting a client**
- A **risk or error that needs immediate attention**
- A **change in a client’s financial health or business activity**
- A **potential issue in an action being taken right now**

Taxonomy enables firms to deliver **timely, personalised, and highly relevant advice**, while also protecting the firm and its clients from risks that would otherwise go unnoticed.

---

# The Problem: The Manual Tax Gap

Tax advisory firms already possess enormous amounts of information:

- Client emails and communications
- Accounting and ERP data
- Tax filings and historical advice
- Government legislation and regulatory guidance
- Public filings and grant databases
- Industry news and external signals

The challenge is not a lack of data.

The problem is that **no human team can continuously monitor and cross-reference all of it — nor verify every action taken across every workflow**.

This creates what Taxonomy calls the **Manual Tax Gap**.

Advisors intuitively know there are things they are missing:

- Advisory opportunities they have not spotted
- Regulatory changes affecting clients
- Risks emerging within client businesses
- Errors or inconsistencies buried in documents
- External events that may affect their firm or client relationships
- Mistakes or risks introduced during everyday work

They simply do not have the time or capacity to analyse everything required.

The result is missed opportunities, but also **unseen risks** — some of which can be financially or reputationally critical.

Taxonomy exists to close this gap.

# What Taxonomy Is

Taxonomy is a **background intelligence layer for tax advisory firms**.

It continuously:

1. Ingests data from across the firm’s ecosystem
2. Structures and connects that information
3. Monitors it through specialised **Sensors**
4. Performs real-time workflow checks through **Interceptors**
5. Generates **Signals** when meaningful events, risks, or opportunities are detected
6. Pushes those Signals directly to advisors

The key principle is **push, not search**.

Advisors do not need to ask questions or check dashboards.

Instead, they receive notifications such as:

> “You should look at this for Client X — here is why.”

Or in the case of an Interceptor:

> “Before sending this to Client X, you should review this issue.”

Signals may relate to:

### Opportunities

- A newly relevant tax relief
- Eligibility for a grant or incentive
- A strategic advisory opportunity

### Client Value

- A legislative or HMRC guidance change affecting a client
- A regulatory development relevant to their structure
- A business event that may require advice

### Risk Detection

- A potential clerical error in a deliverable
- A filing inconsistency
- A client approaching a risk threshold
- A risky statement or incorrect claim in a client communication

### External Signals

- A client’s financial performance deteriorating
- A company entering administration
- Early indicators of client churn
- Market or regulatory changes affecting the firm

Signals are delivered through the tools advisors already use:

- Email
- Slack
- CRM systems
- Practice management software

Interceptors may also operate **directly inside those tools**, checking actions as they occur.

The advisor can then drill into the underlying evidence and sources if needed.

# The Three Intelligence Layers

Taxonomy creates a full advisory context by combining three types of information.

## 1. Private Nervous System

The firm’s internal data:

- Emails
- Accounting software integrations (e.g. Xero, QuickBooks)
- Tax filings
- CRM systems
- Historical advisory work
- Internal deliverables and documents

This captures the **real operational and advisory context** of each client relationship.

Interceptors can also operate within these environments — for example checking outbound emails, deliverables, or filings before they are sent.

---

## 2. Regulatory Bedrock

Authoritative regulatory data including:

- Tax legislation
- HMRC guidance
- Case law
- Regulatory updates

This ensures all Signals remain **grounded in current legal reality**.

---

## 3. Public Horizon

External signals such as:

- Companies House filings
- Grant databases
- Company websites
- LinkedIn activity
- Media coverage
- Public financial indicators

These signals provide **early visibility into changes happening outside the firm’s systems**.

The power comes from **cross-referencing all three layers simultaneously**.

For example:

- A legislative change (Regulatory Bedrock)
- Cross-referenced with a client’s accounts (Private Nervous System)
- And a recent Companies House filing (Public Horizon)

A Sensor monitoring these relationships can generate a Signal highlighting the resulting advisory implication.

An Interceptor may use the same context to verify whether a specific action — such as advice being sent to a client — is consistent with that context.

# Beyond Opportunity: Continuous Advisory Awareness

Taxonomy is not just about finding new advisory work.

It provides **continuous awareness of what matters across the firm’s entire client base and workflows**.

This intelligence generally falls into three categories of Signals.

### Opportunity Signals

Sensors detect opportunities for valuable advisory work that were previously hidden in the data.

---

### Value Signals

Sensors detect changes that allow advisors to proactively inform clients when something affects them — such as:

- legislative changes
- regulatory updates
- industry developments

These Signals strengthen the advisor’s role as a **trusted strategic partner**.

---

### Risk Signals

Sensors and Interceptors detect risks that could affect:

- the client
- the firm
- the advisory relationship

Examples include:

- financial deterioration or administration risk
- clerical or calculation errors
- filing inconsistencies
- operational risks inside the firm
- early signals of potential client churn
- risky or incorrect statements before they are sent to a client

Some Signals may be **mission-critical**, allowing the firm to intervene before a problem escalates.

# The Technical Foundation

Taxonomy structures information using a **proprietary knowledge graph**.

This system organises data into a structured network of relationships between:

- companies
- ownership structures
- financial movements
- tax legislation
- advisory history
- regulatory rules

Sensors and Interceptors operate on top of this structured graph.

Sensors continuously analyse relationships and changes across the data.

Interceptors use the same contextual model to evaluate **specific actions occurring inside workflows**, such as sending an email, producing a deliverable, or submitting a filing.

Alongside this, semantic processing handles unstructured information such as:

- emails
- documents
- web content

This hybrid architecture ensures that Signals are:

- highly relevant
- fully cited
- traceable back to their source

In tax and professional services, this transparency is essential:

**if an insight cannot be cited, it cannot be relied upon.**

# The Platform Model

Taxonomy is an **extensible intelligence platform**, not a rigid SaaS tool.

The platform operates through a growing network of intelligence modules.

## Sensors

Sensors continuously monitor data sources and relationships across the firm's ecosystem, generating Signals when meaningful patterns or changes are detected.

## Interceptors

Interceptors operate **inside workflows**, acting as real-time checkpoints that verify actions before they occur.

Examples include:

- checking outbound client emails for risky statements
- reviewing deliverables for inconsistencies
- verifying claims against regulatory guidance
- flagging potential filing errors before submission

Both Sensors and Interceptors produce **Signals** when they detect something that requires attention.

Firms can deploy:

- pre-built modules for common advisory scenarios
- customised modules tailored to their advisory specialisms

For deeper implementations, Forward Deployed Engineers work directly with firms to build bespoke Sensors and Interceptors aligned with their methodology.

Over time, this embeds Taxonomy deeply into the firm’s advisory infrastructure.

# The Value Proposition

Most professional software focuses on **efficiency** — doing the same work faster.

Taxonomy focuses on **awareness and intelligence**.

By continuously running Sensors across the firm’s data ecosystem and deploying Interceptors within workflows, Taxonomy ensures that important Signals are never missed — and critical mistakes are caught before they happen.

This helps firms:

- discover new advisory opportunities
- deliver proactive value to clients
- identify risks before they escalate
- prevent errors before they reach clients
- stay aware of changes affecting their clients and business

For advisors, this unlocks new revenue and strengthens client relationships.

For clients, it means receiving **timely guidance, protection from risk, and better financial outcomes**.

The greatest potential of modern data intelligence in professional services is not automating existing work.

It is enabling firms to **see what they could never previously see — and catch what they could never previously catch**.

Opportunities that were buried in data.  
Risks that were impossible to monitor manually.  
Signals that would otherwise go unnoticed.  
Errors that would only be discovered after the fact.

Taxonomy turns those hidden signals into **actionable Signals surfaced to the right advisor at the right moment**.

Once advisors experience a system that continuously monitors their entire client base and quietly safeguards their workflows, the old reactive way of working becomes unthinkable.

Taxonomy is building the infrastructure for the **proactive, aware, and continuously informed tax firm**.
